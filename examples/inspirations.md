# Inspirations: Stylistic References & Mood Boards

This guide helps you gather and document design inspiration for your product.

---

## What are Inspirations?

Inspirations are visual and stylistic references that guide your product's design direction. They help communicate the desired look, feel, and aesthetic to your team and stakeholders.

**Purpose:**
- Align team on visual direction
- Communicate design intent
- Guide design decisions
- Set aesthetic tone
- Inspire creativity
- Avoid starting from scratch

**Types:** Websites, apps, UI patterns, color palettes, typography, illustrations, animations

---

## How to Gather Inspiration

### 1. Define Your Design Goals

Before collecting inspiration, identify what you need:

**Questions to ask:**
- What emotions should the design evoke? (calm, energetic, professional, playful)
- Who is the target audience? (professionals, students, creatives, general public)
- What's the primary use case? (productivity, entertainment, education, commerce)
- What's the brand personality? (modern, traditional, bold, minimal, warm, cool)

### 3. Analyze Competitors & Similar Products

**Study direct competitors:**
- What works well?
- What feels outdated?
- What differentiates them?
- What can you do better?

**Study adjacent products:**
- Different industry, similar patterns
- What design patterns do users already know?

## Example 1: SaaS Productivity App (Taskly)

### Design Direction
**Goal:** Clean, focused, calm productivity tool that reduces overwhelm

**Keywords:** Minimal, spacious, calm, professional, modern

---

### Visual References

#### Overall Aesthetic
- **Linear (linear.app)** — Clean, minimal UI with subtle animations
- **Height (height.app)** — Calm color palette, excellent typography
- **Notion** — Flexible layouts, beautiful database views
- **Things 3** — Elegant task management, smooth interactions

**What to borrow:**
- Minimal, distraction-free interfaces
- Subtle hover states and micro-interactions
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Generous whitespace

---

#### Navigation Patterns
- **Superhuman** — Command palette for keyboard shortcuts
- **Raycast** — Fast search-first interface
- **Slack** — Sidebar navigation with clear sections

**What to borrow:**
- Collapsible sidebar
- Quick switcher (Cmd+K)
- Clear active states

---

#### Color Palette Inspiration
- **Height:** Soft blues and grays
- **Linear:** Dark mode with accent colors
- **Basecamp:** Warm, friendly colors

**Our Direction:**
- Primary: Soft blue (#3B82F6)
- Background: Light gray (#F9FAFB)
- Text: Dark gray (#1F2937)
- Accent: Green for success (#10B981)

---

#### Typography
- **Notion:** Inter font family (clean, modern)
- **Linear:** System fonts (fast, native feel)
- **Arc Browser:** Custom geometric sans-serif

**Our Choice:**
- Font: Inter (Google Fonts)
- Weights: 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (Semibold)

---

#### Illustrations & Empty States
- **Dropbox Paper:** Simple line illustrations
- **Notion:** Friendly empty states
- **Linear:** Minimal icons, no heavy illustrations

**Our Approach:**
- Lucide icons (consistent, minimal)
- Simple empty states with friendly copy
- No heavy illustrations (keep it professional)

## Example 2: E-commerce Platform (Shopnest)

### Design Direction
**Goal:** Modern, trustworthy e-commerce with focus on product photography

**Keywords:** Clean, bold typography, product-first, conversion-focused

---

### Visual References

#### Overall Aesthetic
- **Shopify** — Clean product pages, clear CTAs
- **Stripe** — Trustworthy, professional design
- **Allbirds** — Minimalist, product-focused
- **Warby Parker** — Bold typography, friendly

**What to borrow:**
- Large product images
- Clear pricing and CTAs
- Trust badges and reviews
- Simple checkout flow

---

#### Product Pages
- **Apple** — Large hero images, minimal copy
- **Nike** — Interactive product viewers
- **ASOS** — Multiple product images, size guides

**Our Approach:**
- Hero image with zoom
- Image gallery (4-6 images)
- Sticky "Add to Cart" button
- Size/variant selector
- Reviews below fold

---

#### Checkout Flow
- **Stripe Checkout** — Single-page, autofill, trust indicators
- **Shopify** — Progress bar, breadcrumbs
- **Amazon** — One-click checkout (for returning users)

**Our Approach:**
- 3-step checkout (Shipping → Payment → Confirm)
- Progress indicator
- Guest checkout option
- Trust badges (secure payment, returns)

## Creating Your Inspiration Board

### Tools for Mood Boards

**Visual Bookmarking:**
- **Pinterest** — Save and organize images
- **Are.na** — More design-focused alternative
- **Raindrop.io** — Bookmark manager with visuals

**Design Tools:**
- **Figma** — Create mood boards in design files
- **Miro** — Collaborative whiteboard
- **Notion** — Embed images and links

**Browser Extensions:**
- **Muz.li** — Design inspiration in new tab
- **Panda** — Curated design news

---

### Mood Board Template (Figma/Miro)

**Sections to include:**

1. **Overall Feel** (3-5 full website screenshots)
2. **Color Palette** (extracted colors from references)
3. **Typography** (font pairings from other sites)
4. **Navigation** (header and sidebar examples)
5. **Components** (buttons, cards, forms)
6. **Illustrations/Images** (visual style examples)
7. **Animations** (links to animated examples)

## Documenting Inspiration

### Format: Link + Description + Why

**Example:**
```markdown
## Navigation Inspiration

### Linear (https://linear.app)
**What:** Collapsible sidebar with clear hierarchy
**Why:** Clean, doesn't overwhelm with options
**Apply:** Use similar sidebar structure, limit to 5-7 main items

### Superhuman (https://superhuman.com)
**What:** Command palette (Cmd+K)
**Why:** Fast navigation for power users
**Apply:** Implement global search/command palette

### Slack (https://slack.com)
**What:** Active state with background color + bold
**Why:** Clear visual feedback
**Apply:** Use similar active states in sidebar
```

- Friendly copy (like Notion)

- Clear CTAs

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### 1. Copying Without Understanding
```
❌ Copying a design blindly
✅ Understanding why it works and adapting to your context
```

### 2. Too Many Inspirations
```
❌ 100 reference images with no direction
✅ 5-10 focused references with clear takeaways
```

### 3. Conflicting Styles
```
❌ Mixing playful illustrations with serious corporate design
✅ Consistent aesthetic across all references
```

### 4. Ignoring Your Users
```
❌ Designing for designers (over-designed)
✅ Designing for your actual users
```
